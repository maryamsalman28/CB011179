Sea turtle protection is the focus of the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery, which is situated in Hikkaduwa, Sri Lanka. It provides tourists an opportunity to learn about the endangered species' conservation efforts while also acting as a key shelter for them. Several species of sea turtles may be seen at the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery. The turtle hatchery is essential for gathering and safeguarding turtle eggs, which are prey to predators and human activity. Visitors may see the remarkable procedure of incubation and even take part inÂ releasing baby turtles into the ocean. Along with the opportunity to see turtles hatch and be released, the hatchery offers educational materials about the life cycle, habitat, and challenges faced by sea turtles. A knowledgeable team is on hand to lead visitors through the facility, providing information on conservation initiatives and responding to inquiries about these majestic animals.
